Preface

Purpose

In order to be an effective programmer, one must have a firm grasp on one of the most basic technologies used in computer science: algorithms. The autodidact surely teaches himself to grapple with algorithms. However, one can not expect that algorithms do not move beyond FizzBuzz. The complexity involved in the application and analysis of these tortuous blocks of code is just as capable of evoking anger as they are awe. What follows is a document of my attempt to achieve a firm grasp of this material.

Plan

The course of this material will run parallel with my endeavor through Introduction to Algorithms, by Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L. Rivest, and Clifford Stein. The pages that follow are in no way a replication of the text book, but a replication of my notes while slowly making my way through the book. For this reason, I highly recommend that any fastidious reader of this material purchase or rent this book. It's a fantastic read. For the sake of brevity, I will refer to this book as the CLRS.

One task that I've taken upon myself is that of translation. Wherever the CLRS has made use of pseudocode, and wherever I choose to show my notes for that material, I've chosen to make that code available in Javascript ES6 and Ruby, as both of these languages pertain to my particular programming efforts. In the future, I might choose to demonstrate this code in other languages, but these two will make for a good start.
